About Me

My name is Phuntsho Norbu and I am from Bumthang, Bhutan. I recently Wheaton graduated with a B.A in Computer Science and Mathematics-Economics from College in Massachusetts, USA. I was recently awarded the Thomas J. Watson Fellowship. This is a year-long grant for students to conduct independent research or exploration outside of the United States in fields that interest them.

This year, starting on july 18th I will be travelling to Tanzania, Madagascar, Brazil and Japan for my Watson Year. My focus for the Watson year is on drone technology and the valuable ways it is currently being used in different parts of the world and across different communities. I will be travelling mostly to rural areas to learn, experience and document how people in such communities have used drones to better lives and most importantly how I can bring such knowledge and application back to Bhutan. My main interest lies in the nexus of technology and data. Hence I want to pursue further studies in this field after my Watson Year.
