About Me

My name is Phuntsho Norbu and I am from Bumthang, Bhutan. I recently graduated with a B.A in Computer Science and Mathematics-Economics from Wheaton College in Massachusetts, USA. I was recently awarded the Thomas J. Watson Fellowship . This is a year-long grant for students to conduct independent research or exploration outside of the United States in fields that interest them.

This year, starting on July 18th I will be travelling to Tanzania, Mongolia, Nepal, Japan and Peru for my Watson Year. My focus is on drone technology and the valuable ways it is currently being used in different parts of the world and across different communities. I will be travelling mostly to rural areas to learn, experience and document how people in such communities have used drones to better lives and most importantly how I can bring such knowledge and application back to Bhutan. My main interest lies in the nexus of technology and data. Hence I want to pursue further studies in this field after my Watson Year.

Power of Local  

My time with Tanzania Flying Labs was one-of-a-kind, and I can genuinely imagine no better place to have started my Watson journey. I was part of this small yet crucial organization, making giant steps towards building robotics capacity in the country. The team allowed me to connect with people from grass root levels to national conservation organizations and see how they are trying to use drones to better their community.
